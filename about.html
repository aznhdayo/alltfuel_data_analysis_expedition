About Evasta

Building clarity for EV charging infrastructure decisions.

  • Electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure in the United States is expanding rapidly.
  • Relevant data can be difficult to access, analyze, and visualize in practical workflows.
  • This platform makes national EV charging insights clearer, more accessible, and more actionable.
  • Primary audiences include city planners, policy makers, researchers, businesses, and everyday EV drivers.
  • Evasta connects public infrastructure data to real planning questions: where growth is accelerating, where gaps remain, and how network distribution changes month by month.

As EV adoption grows, charging infrastructure planning becomes more than a technical exercise—it becomes a public-access, energy-equity, and transportation-readiness challenge. The purpose of this page is to explain how Evasta organizes that complexity into practical insights that are easier to trust and act on.

Our Mission

  • Turn raw federal EV charging data into meaningful insights.
  • Provide practical tools for planning and decision-making.
  • Support transparent, evidence-based infrastructure discussions across public and private stakeholders.
  • Reduce the time between data collection and practical action.

Our mission is focused on translation: taking large, technical datasets and converting them into clear, interpretable patterns that support real-world decisions for expansion strategy, policy prioritization, and service reliability.

What This Platform Covers

  • Uses the U.S. Department of Energy AFDC monthly station dataset.
  • State-level charging summaries with comparable metrics.
  • EV network/operator comparisons to assess market structure.
  • Plug counts and network-by-state matrices for capacity visibility.
  • California-specific county and network analytics for localized planning.
  • Month-over-month trend tracking to identify momentum shifts.
  • Coverage views that reveal concentration, imbalance, and expansion corridors.
  • Practical summaries that support both executive overviews and analytical deep dives.

This platform is built to bridge national and local perspectives. It provides the breadth needed for policy and funding context, while still preserving enough granularity to support county-level and corridor-level questions.

Why It Helps

  • AFDC data is comprehensive but large and complex for many users.
  • Transforms raw national data into monthly plug-count analytics.
  • Provides state-level and network-level comparisons.
  • Adds California county-level summaries and trend visualizations.
  • Highlights where infrastructure investment appears to be keeping pace—or falling behind—demand signals.
  • Fills a public gap with a transparent, easy-to-use platform built on open federal data.

Better visibility leads to better decisions. By simplifying how infrastructure data is read and compared, Evasta helps teams spend less time reconciling spreadsheets and more time deciding where to build next.

Who this Project was made for

  • Counties and regional planners.
  • Clean Cities Coalitions.
  • State energy offices.
  • Transportation researchers and academic teams.
  • EV charging companies and ecosystem partners.
  • Journalists, analysts, and civic data advocates.
  • Anyone interested in EV infrastructure growth.
  • Supports both high-level national trends and practical, actionable analysis.

Whether the goal is policy design, grant planning, infrastructure deployment, or public communication, this project is designed to provide a shared factual baseline that different audiences can interpret quickly.
